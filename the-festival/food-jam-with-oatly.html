The Festival

Whereas The Conference is two days of super-curated talks at the Opera, The Festival is what we’d call “an intense smorgasbord” of workshops, seminars, food, culture, music, master classes and festivities from which you can customize your own experience. All signed, sealed and delivered by speakers from The Conference and brilliant minds from our community.

Most festival events will be for free and anyone is welcome to register. It doesn’t matter if you’re attending The Conference or not. The Festival is for everyone interested in the now and beyond.

LOVE/HATE ONLINE: Digitalised suppression techniques and confirmation STRATEGIES workshop

Together with Winnet we'll explore how suppression techniques are used online today.

Location: Media Evolution City
More info

During the 1970s, the Norweigan social psychologist Berit Ås revised and popularized the theory of suppression techniques. Since then, new social arenas have changed where and how we communicate.

As a result, suppression techniques and confirmation strategies are now used IRL as well as online. During this interactive workshop with Winnet Skåne, we’re going to explore how suppression techniques are used online today, as well as trying to forecast whether or not new ones will arise because of the development of social media.

Registration opens August 1

What You'll Learn

  • All you ever wanted to know about the history of suppression techniques
  • How suppression techniques are used today
  • How the future of suppression techniques might take shape

Join The Ground to explore where, and what happens when people and technology meet.

Location: The Ground
More info

Where, exactly, do people and technology meet? And more importantly: what happens when they do?

The Ground presents their celebrated Tech & Ethics Talks, a monthly talk series aimed about the implications and consequences of tech’s relation to ethics. The idea is to encourage individuals and companies to think more about their roles – not only within business ecosystems, but in society as a well. The Ground wants people to reflect upon how their products might affect customers and larger social processes, particularly with regards to long-term consequences.

Lunch from Susanna Bill is included.

Registration opens August 1.

What You'll Learn

  • Quite a lot about how tech and ethics are intertwined
  • To reflect on your relation to new technology
12:00 pm - 1:30 pm

Lunch and Game of Threats™ with PwC workshop

Come try out a digital game designed to simulate the speed and complexity of an actual cyber breach.

Location: PwC
More info

Come join this session of Game of Threats, a digital game designed to simulate the speed and complexity of an actual cyber breach.

The solution integrates elements of gamification and game theory to provide an interactive experience where one team tries to defend itself from a believable cyber threat. During the course of the game, a healthy lunch will also be served to the players between the sessions.

The game forces players to make choices about how to attack (if playing the threat actor) and defend (if playing the hypothetical company at breach) with limited information and to balance investment in capability and responding to the other teams’ actions and plans. PwC moderators will facilitate a direct dialogue with clients about their choices during the game, as well as providing on the spot feedback about strategy and decision-making. This approach elevates the impact of Client investments in cybersecurity awareness through real-time feedback on their selected actions, and a discussion about alternate responses and their potential outcomes.

Registration opens August 1.

What You'll Learn

  • Learn lessons about your organisation’s ability to respond to a cyber attack
  • Learn key cyber security trends and terminology
  • Spark a leadership discussion about your cybersecurity readiness
